Rosa Parks

The first lady of civil rights

Achievements

  • 1913-Born Rosa Louise McCauley in Tuskegee, Alabama. Married Raymond Parks, a barber from Montgomery in 1932
  • 1943-In December 1943, Parks became active in the civil rights movement, joined the Montgomery chapter of the NAACP, and was elected secretary.
  • 1955-December 1, 1955, Rosa made a stand by refusing to give up her seat. Blake said, "Why don't you stand up?" Parks responded, "I don't think I should have to stand up." Blake called the police to arrest Parks.
